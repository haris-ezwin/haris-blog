For a long time my world was one classroom. The shift to running a business didn't change how much I care — it changed what I had to care about.

## One classroom

As a teacher, success was simple and immediate: did this student understand, did they improve, did they leave more confident than they came. The feedback loop was tight and deeply satisfying.

## The whole thing

As a founder, the unit of care got bigger. Now it's every classroom, every tutor, every parent who trusts us with their kid — plus payroll, growth, and the thousand decisions that never reach a student directly but shape every one of them.

> You stop optimising for the best lesson and start optimising for the best thousand lessons you'll never personally teach.

## Holding both

The trap is letting the business swallow the teaching, or letting the teaching keep you from building. The job is holding both — staying close enough to the classroom to know what good looks like, while building the thing that makes good happen at scale.

Both matter more than ever. That tension is the work.
